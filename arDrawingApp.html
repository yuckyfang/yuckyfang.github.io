AR Drawing Web App

(Case Study in Progress)

Scenario

This is a NSF funded research project where my professor, Ron Eglash, combines STEM, culture, and art to create a web app that kids can trace cultural designs they have coded online with, onto paper crafts, body art, and textiles with Augmented Reality.

Design Process

Finding Target Audience
"Kids" was a very broad target audience so with the consultation with my professor, we decided to narrow down and focus on middle school and high school students as many teachers partnered with this project taught those grades.

Interviews
With the target users in mind, I interviewed 10 middle school and high school students about what they would like in an app that lets them trace anything they want on any surface.

Market Research
To learn more about different AR drawing apps on the market, I utilized competitive analysis to gauge how AR drawing apps are designed and developed, what the users look for and expect, as well as what users like and dislike about their experiences.

UX for AR Research
Since AR is an emerging technology and one that is heavily dependent on the experience, I made sure to research lots about UX for designing AR applications. I read many AR Design guidelines from Google, Microsoft, Unity, Magic Leap, Apple, SnapChat, and Facebook as well as watched conference videos and talks about the evolving design. I created a webpage on my findings here.
